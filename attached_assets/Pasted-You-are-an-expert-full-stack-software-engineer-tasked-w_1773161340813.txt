You are an expert full-stack software engineer tasked with building the MVP of a fintech web application called Aventris – Wealth Wellness Hub.

Your goal is to design and implement a working prototype that satisfies the requirements of a fintech hackathon problem statement.

The application should be clean, modern, and focused on financial risk intelligence and wealth wellness analytics.

The system must transform fragmented financial asset data into a clear financial health dashboard.

This product is NOT a trading platform, NOT a robo advisor, and NOT a stock recommendation engine.

Instead, it is a risk transparency and financial wellness analytics platform.

Aventris is a Wealth Wellness Hub that aggregates a user’s financial assets and analyzes their financial health using portfolio analytics, diversification metrics, liquidity analysis, and stress testing.

The system should help users understand:

• how diversified their wealth is
• their exposure to market indices
• their liquidity strength
• potential downside in stress scenarios
• the structural resilience of their wealth

The product should translate complex financial metrics into clear, intuitive insights.

Users currently manage wealth across fragmented platforms such as:

• brokerage portfolios
• crypto wallets
• bank savings
• private investments

Because of fragmentation, investors cannot clearly see:

• their real diversification
• their macro exposure
• their liquidity strength
• their overall financial health

Aventris solves this by creating a Wealth Wallet + Financial Health Dashboard.

Focus only on the Minimum Viable Product.

Do not overengineer.

⸻

1. Wealth Wallet (Asset Aggregation)

Users should be able to input their assets.

For the MVP, use manual input rather than broker integrations.

Users should be able to add:

Asset Name
Asset Type (Stock / ETF / Crypto / Cash / Private Asset)
Value
Quantity
Sector (for stocks/ETFs)

Example assets:

AAPL
NVDA
BTC
Cash savings
Real estate value

Store portfolio data in a simple database or in session memory.

2. Wealth Composition Visualization

Display visual breakdowns of the user’s wealth.

Charts should include:

• Asset allocation pie chart
• Sector distribution
• Asset class breakdown

Use a clean dashboard layout.

3. Wealth Health Score

Calculate a single score representing overall financial wellness.

Score range: 0–100

The score should combine:

Diversification Score
Liquidity Score
Risk Exposure Score

Example output:

Wealth Health Score: 64 / 100

Provide simple explanations.

Example:

“Your portfolio shows moderate diversification but high exposure to technology equities.”

⸻

4. Diversification Analysis

Compute diversification metrics based on asset allocation.

Indicators include:

• concentration by sector
• concentration by asset class
• distribution across holdings

Create a Diversification Score (0–100).

Example explanation:

“78% of your portfolio is concentrated in technology equities.”

⸻

5. Correlation and Market Exposure

For stock and ETF assets, calculate exposure to major indices.

Use historical price data if possible.

Otherwise simulate correlations.

Show:

Correlation to S&P 500
Correlation to Nasdaq

Example output:

“Your portfolio is highly correlated with the Nasdaq (0.89).”

⸻

6. Liquidity Analysis

Determine how much of the user’s wealth is liquid.

Liquid assets:

• cash
• stocks
• ETFs
• crypto

Illiquid assets:

• real estate
• private investments

Calculate:

Liquidity Ratio

Example:

Liquid Assets: $85,000
Total Wealth: $120,000
Liquidity Ratio: 71%

Explain in simple terms.

⸻

7. Stress Test Simulator

Allow users to simulate market events.

Example scenarios:

Scenario 1 – Global Market Crash (-20% equities)

Scenario 2 – Technology Sector Crash (-30% tech stocks)

Scenario 3 – Crypto Market Crash (-40%)

Estimate potential portfolio impact.

Example output:

“Under a technology sector crash scenario, your portfolio value would decline approximately 24%.”

⸻
